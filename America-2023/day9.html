Day 9 - T-shirts and shorts in an ice rink

We needed a day off, so we lazed around the ranch in the morning. Some reading, some packing, some planning, some chat with friends and some Dance Moms.

We aimed to arrive mid-afternoon for a public session at the rink. After a bit of stress trying to understand the parking meters, Jake found a spot in the rink’s car park. The rink had a Bradford vibe to it. There were about 8 people on when we arrived and maybe 20 when we left. Jake skating around a bit in some hire skates while Freya plucked up the courage to do some tricks. She said the ice was “plastic” and rubbish, but she built enough confidence to do a few things. Then said the ice was good as it made her spins faster. A coach had a chat with her about where she was from and what skating she did. Me and Alison stayed, sat behind the plastiglass in shorts and not really that cold.

We were out at teatime so ate out at Denny’s because it had a big menu and easy parking. The service was great and the food was huge. There were unlimited refills and when you got three-quarters down your drink, a new one appeared. We were all full.

All except Alison went in the pool for a last hurrah. The kids jumped off the diving board. As dusk descended, the bugs got a bit bitey, so we moved to the hot tub for a last last hurrah, this time with all of us in. Jake lasted half a cycle of jets, Alison to the end, Freya a few minutes into the second one but I held out, drinking the Smirnoff Ice cans that Freya bought but didn’t like.

We have an early start so were all packed and ready to go before having a relatively early night. That chill day was what we needed.
